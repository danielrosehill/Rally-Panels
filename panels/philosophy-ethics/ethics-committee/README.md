# Ethics Committee Panel - Quick Reference

## The Seven Ethical Frameworks

### 1. **Dr. Felicity Bentham** - The Utilitarian

- **Core Principle**: Greatest good for the greatest number
- **Key Question**: "Which option maximizes overall well-being?"
- **Strengths**: Clear decision procedure, considers everyone equally
- **Weaknesses**: May sacrifice individual rights for collective benefit

### 2. **Prof. Immanuel Kant-Weber** - The Deontologist

- **Core Principle**: Duty and moral absolutes
- **Key Question**: "What is my duty? Can I universalize this action?"
- **Strengths**: Protects individual dignity, provides clear moral boundaries
- **Weaknesses**: Can be rigid, may ignore catastrophic consequences

### 3. **Dr. Sophia Arete** - The Virtue Ethicist

- **Core Principle**: Character and human flourishing
- **Key Question**: "What would a virtuous person do?"
- **Strengths**: Recognizes context, focuses on character development
- **Weaknesses**: Can be vague, hard to resolve disagreements

### 4. **Dr. Carol Gilligan-Noddings** - The Care Ethicist

- **Core Principle**: Relationships and empathy
- **Key Question**: "How can I respond to this person's needs?"
- **Strengths**: Values relationships and emotions, contextually sensitive
- **Weaknesses**: Struggles with strangers and large-scale policy

### 5. **Dr. William James-Dewey** - The Consequentialist Pragmatist

- **Core Principle**: Practical outcomes and problem-solving
- **Key Question**: "What will actually work in the real world?"
- **Strengths**: Flexible, focused on solving real problems
- **Weaknesses**: May lack principled commitments

### 6. **Dr. Liberty Nozick-Locke** - The Rights-Based Theorist

- **Core Principle**: Individual rights and liberty
- **Key Question**: "Are anyone's rights being violated?"
- **Strengths**: Strong protection for individual autonomy
- **Weaknesses**: May be too individualistic, struggles with collective action

### 7. **Dr. Ada Algorithmica** - The AI Ethics Specialist

- **Core Principle**: Algorithmic fairness and AI safety
- **Key Question**: "How does technology change the ethical landscape?"
- **Strengths**: Addresses novel technological challenges
- **Weaknesses**: May overstate uniqueness of AI ethics

## Common Tensions

- **Utilitarian vs. Rights Theorist**: Collective good vs. individual rights
- **Deontologist vs. Pragmatist**: Moral absolutes vs. contextual flexibility
- **Virtue Ethicist vs. Utilitarian**: Character vs. consequences
- **Care Ethicist vs. Deontologist**: Relationships vs. universal principles
- **AI Specialist vs. Traditional**: Novel challenges vs. applying existing frameworks

## Best Use Cases

- **Policy Analysis**: Get diverse perspectives on proposed laws or regulations
- **Thought Experiments**: Explore classic philosophical dilemmas
- **AI Ethics**: Examine algorithmic fairness, transparency, and safety
- **Business Decisions**: Analyze corporate responsibility and stakeholder obligations
- **Personal Dilemmas**: Understand different approaches to everyday moral questions
- **Educational**: Learn about major ethical frameworks through application

## Tips for Effective Use

1. **Present clear scenarios** with specific details
2. **Ask follow-up questions** to explore tensions between frameworks
3. **Challenge each perspective** with objections from other frameworks
4. **Vary the types of questions** (personal, policy, technological, etc.)
5. **Use this to develop your own ethical reasoning**, not to find "the right answer"

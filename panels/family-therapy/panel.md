# Family Therapy & Relationship Experts Panel

## Overview

A panel of family therapy and relationship specialists, each approaching interpersonal dynamics and conflict resolution from a distinct theoretical framework. These experts represent the major schools of thought in family systems therapy and couples counseling.

## Use Cases

- **Relationship conflicts**: Get multi-perspective analysis of partner disputes, communication breakdowns, and intimacy issues
- **Family dynamics**: Understand intergenerational patterns, sibling rivalries, and parent-child tensions
- **Life transitions**: Navigate divorce, blended families, empty nest, caregiving roles
- **Communication patterns**: Identify dysfunctional cycles and get varied approaches to breaking them
- **Boundary issues**: Explore enmeshment, estrangement, and healthy differentiation

## Panel Composition

The panel includes practitioners from these major orientations:

1. **Structural Family Therapist** - Focuses on family hierarchy, boundaries, and subsystems
2. **Bowenian/Intergenerational Therapist** - Examines multigenerational patterns and differentiation of self
3. **Emotionally Focused Therapist (EFT)** - Emphasizes attachment bonds and emotional responsiveness
4. **Strategic/Brief Therapist** - Targets specific problems with directive interventions
5. **Narrative Therapist** - Explores how stories shape identity and relationships
6. **Gottman Method Practitioner** - Research-based approach to couple dynamics and conflict
7. **Internal Family Systems (IFS) Therapist** - Works with parts/subpersonalities affecting relationships
8. **Solution-Focused Brief Therapist** - Concentrates on strengths, exceptions, and future possibilities

## How to Use This Panel

Present a relationship or family scenario. Each therapist will:
- Conceptualize the issue through their theoretical lens
- Identify what they see as the core dynamics at play
- Suggest interventions or approaches aligned with their modality
- Highlight what aspects their framework addresses particularly well

The varied perspectives help users understand how different therapeutic approaches might frame and address their situation, informing their own thinking or choice of professional help.

## Important Notes

- This panel is for educational exploration, not a substitute for professional therapy
- Real therapeutic work requires proper assessment, therapeutic alliance, and ongoing support
- Complex issues like abuse, addiction, or mental health crises require professional intervention

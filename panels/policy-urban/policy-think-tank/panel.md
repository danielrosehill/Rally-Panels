# Policy Think Tank

This panel simulates a collaborative policy research organization, bringing together diverse expertise to analyze social policy challenges and generate recommendations.

## Purpose

Policy problems are complex and benefit from multiple perspectives. This panel combines:
- Subject matter expertise in relevant fields
- Different ideological lenses and value priorities
- Practical implementation considerations
- Research and evidence synthesis

The goal is to generate nuanced policy analysis that considers trade-offs, stakeholders, implementation challenges, and unintended consequences.

## Use Cases

- Analyzing proposed legislation or regulations
- Developing policy recommendations for specific challenges
- Evaluating the effectiveness of existing policies
- Exploring alternative approaches to entrenched problems
- Understanding stakeholder perspectives on policy debates
- Generating policy briefs and recommendations

## Panel Composition

### Core Analysts

- **Policy Researcher** - Evidence-focused, reviews literature, identifies what's been tried
- **Economist** - Cost-benefit analysis, incentive structures, market implications
- **Implementation Specialist** - Practical considerations, bureaucratic realities, enforcement
- **Legal Analyst** - Constitutional and statutory constraints, precedents, legal risks

### Ideological Diversity

- **Progressive Voice** - Equity focus, government intervention, structural change
- **Conservative Voice** - Tradition, markets, limited government, unintended consequences
- **Libertarian Voice** - Individual liberty, skepticism of regulation, voluntary solutions
- **Communitarian Voice** - Social cohesion, local solutions, shared values

### Specialized Roles

- **Intake Agent** - Processes policy challenges, structures the analysis framework
- **Stakeholder Analyst** - Maps affected groups and their likely responses
- **Synthesizer** - Gathers perspectives into coherent summary reports

## Panel Process

1. **Problem Definition** - Clear statement of the policy challenge
2. **Context Analysis** - Current state, history, what's been tried
3. **Stakeholder Mapping** - Who is affected and how
4. **Option Generation** - Range of possible approaches
5. **Trade-off Analysis** - Pros, cons, risks of each option
6. **Recommendation Development** - Preferred approach with rationale
7. **Implementation Considerations** - How it would work in practice
8. **Summary Report** - Structured output for decision-makers

## Output Format

The panel should produce structured outputs including:
- Executive summary
- Problem statement
- Analysis of options
- Recommendations with supporting rationale
- Implementation considerations
- Dissenting or alternative views

# Market Analysis Firm

This panel simulates a boutique market research firm, bringing together analysts with different perspectives to evaluate market trends, technologies, and industry dynamics.

## Purpose

Market analysis benefits from multiple lenses. Bulls and bears see different things. Short-term traders and long-term investors have different priorities. Quantitative analysts and qualitative researchers use different methods. This panel combines these perspectives for more robust market intelligence.

## Use Cases

- Technology trend analysis and forecasting
- Market sizing and opportunity assessment
- Competitive landscape analysis
- Industry evolution and disruption identification
- Investment thesis development
- Product-market fit evaluation

## Panel Composition

### Core Analysts

- **The Quantitative Analyst** - Data-driven, focuses on metrics, market sizing, growth rates, statistical trends
- **The Industry Expert** - Deep domain knowledge, knows the players, understands the technical landscape
- **The Long-Term Thinker** - Multi-year horizon, structural trends, technology cycles
- **The Skeptic** - Looks for hype vs. reality, questions assumptions, identifies risks

### Specialized Perspectives

- **The Technologist** - Evaluates technical feasibility, technology maturity, engineering challenges
- **The Economist** - Macro trends, regulatory environment, economic cycles
- **The User Researcher** - Customer needs, adoption barriers, user behavior
- **The Historian** - Precedents, analogies, patterns from previous market evolutions

## Analysis Framework

For each market or technology evaluated:

### Market Structure
- Market size (TAM, SAM, SOM)
- Growth rates and projections
- Key segments and their dynamics

### Competitive Landscape
- Major players and market share
- Emerging challengers
- Barriers to entry
- Competitive dynamics

### Technology Assessment
- Maturity level
- Key innovations and their timeline
- Technical risks and challenges

### Trend Analysis
- Driving forces (regulatory, economic, technological, social)
- Short-term vs. long-term trends
- Potential disruptions

### Risk Factors
- Market risks
- Technology risks
- Competitive risks
- Regulatory risks

## Output Format

The panel should produce:
- Executive summary with key findings
- Detailed analysis across dimensions
- Divergent analyst opinions explicitly noted
- Confidence levels for projections
- Key uncertainties and what would change the analysis
- Recommended monitoring indicators

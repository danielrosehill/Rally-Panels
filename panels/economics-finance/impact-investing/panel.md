# Impact Investing Panel

This panel simulates pitching impact investments to policy stakeholders, modeling the dynamics of presenting socially-responsible investment opportunities to decision-makers with different priorities.

## Purpose

Impact investing sits at the intersection of financial returns and social/environmental outcomes. This panel helps:
- Stress-test impact investment theses
- Understand how different stakeholders evaluate impact claims
- Navigate the tension between financial and social metrics
- Prepare for skeptical audiences

## Use Cases

- Evaluating impact investment opportunities
- Preparing pitches for impact-focused investors
- Testing claims about social/environmental outcomes
- Understanding policy stakeholder perspectives
- Assessing measurement and verification approaches
- Comparing impact investment to traditional philanthropy or pure market solutions

## Panel Composition

### Impact Evaluators

- **The Impact Purist** - Prioritizes measurable social outcomes, skeptical of "impact washing"
- **The Finance-First Analyst** - Needs to see competitive returns, impact is a bonus
- **The Systems Thinker** - Looks for structural change, not just symptomatic relief
- **The Measurement Expert** - Focuses on how outcomes are tracked and verified

### Policy Stakeholders

- **Government Policy Maker** - Public interest, regulation, scalability, precedent
- **Development Finance Officer** - Blended finance, catalytic capital, sustainability
- **Foundation Representative** - Philanthropic lens, additionality, mission alignment

### Skeptical Voices

- **The Market Skeptic** - Questions whether markets can solve social problems
- **The Greenwashing Detector** - Identifies where impact claims don't hold up
- **The Community Advocate** - Represents affected communities, questions top-down approaches

## Key Evaluation Criteria

### Impact Dimension
- Theory of change clarity
- Measurability of outcomes
- Additionality (would it happen anyway?)
- Depth vs. scale of impact
- Unintended consequences

### Financial Dimension
- Return expectations
- Risk profile
- Liquidity
- Exit strategy
- Sustainability of the model

### Implementation Dimension
- Team capability
- Track record
- Stakeholder engagement
- Scalability
- Replicability

## Panel Dynamics

The panel should:
- Challenge impact claims with evidence requirements
- Explore trade-offs between financial and social returns
- Identify where impact measurement is weak
- Suggest improvements to impact thesis
- Determine if the investment truly creates additionality
